About

For the past 3 years, I've held various roles at Kabam. These roles have ranged from primarily engineering to a mix of engineering and management. Having already built and launched several products using ruby and rails at my previous company, I was hired at Kabam to help launch a brand new game on Facebook's platform. During this period, I dramatically improved my skills in ruby, rails, mysql, redis, and node. Additionally, as the game expanded to multiple platforms, I gained experience in scaling and api integration. Primarily based on the success I had stabilizing performance and shipping several large features, I was promoted to Senior Engineering Manager and charged with leading both frontend and backend on two successful web games. In this role, I reversed a downward revenue trajectory, stabilized engineering turnover, and instilled a team culture that increased morale and job satisfaction. On the technical side, I also increased performance and throughput on a game that serves over 180,000 requests per minute.

While at Kabam, I realized that I really enjoy building "things". 3 years ago, I would have said these "things" are primarily technical tasks. I've always enjoyed digging deep into a problem and eventually coming up with a solution that scales and provides a solid foundation for future changes. After spending over a year managing two different engineering teams, I've realized that building a team can be just as challenging and rewarding as building a product. I enjoy mentoring junior engineers and guiding them toward simpler and cleaner solutions. I was heavily involved in recruiting and hiring both junior and senior engineers, and I used that experience to build a very strong team and culture. That said, I also really enjoy the days where I can put on headphones and hack on a new feature or idea.

Looking forward to my next role, I'm hoping to leverage my 5+ years of rails experience and 3+ years of engineering management experience to build something new and interesting. I get really excited about solving difficult problems; these can often be overwhelming, but I think that's what makes them so appealing.
